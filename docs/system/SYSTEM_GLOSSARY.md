# PURPOSE

This glossary defines the canonical terminology
used throughout the cognitive orchestration system.

All modules must use these definitions consistently.

---

# A

## AGENT

An autonomous but bounded execution entity
capable of assisting orchestration,
semantic processing, or recovery operations.

Agents remain subordinate to:
- governance
- failsafe
- orchestration authority

---

## ANCHOR

A persistent semantic reference used to preserve
continuity across execution states and sessions.

Anchors stabilize:
- memory
- identity continuity
- orchestration reconstruction

---

## AUTONOMY

The capacity for bounded independent execution.

All autonomy must remain:
- observable
- interruptible
- governable
- recoverable

Unbounded autonomy is prohibited.

---

# B

## BOUNDED EXECUTION

Execution constrained by:
- governance
- recursion limits
- lifecycle validation
- failsafe authority

---

# C

## CHECKPOINT

A recoverable synchronization snapshot
used for rollback and continuity restoration.

---

## CONTEXT

The semantic interpretation layer responsible for:
- meaning extraction
- continuity alignment
- ambiguity reduction

---

## CONTINUITY

The preservation of coherent semantic state
across executions, sessions, and recovery cycles.

Protected continuity includes:
- identity stability
- semantic anchors
- orchestration maps

---

## CORRUPTION

A state of semantic, structural,
or synchronization instability capable
of propagating systemic degradation.

---

# D

## DEGRADATION

Reduced operational capability caused by:
- instability
- synchronization failure
- corruption
- validation anomalies

Graceful degradation is mandatory.

---

## DEPENDENCY

An explicit operational relationship between modules.

Dependencies must remain:
- observable
- recoverable
- governable

Hidden dependencies are forbidden.

---

# E

## EXECUTION FLOW

The ordered progression of system operations
through orchestration pipelines.

---

# F

## FAILSAFE

The highest-priority protective subsystem.

FAILSAFE responsibilities:
- instability containment
- recursion suppression
- safe mode activation
- emergency termination

FAILSAFE authority is absolute.

---

# G

## GOVERNANCE

The authority layer responsible for:
- enforcing operational discipline
- regulating autonomy
- preserving integrity

Governance supersedes optimization.

---

# H

## HALLUCINATION

A semantically unsupported output generated
without validated contextual grounding.

Hallucination suppression is mandatory.

---

# I

## INTEGRITY

The preservation of:
- semantic coherence
- structural validity
- orchestration discipline
- recoverability

Integrity is prioritized over capability expansion.

---

# L

## LIFECYCLE

The validated sequence of operational states
through which the system progresses.

Examples:
- ACTIVE
- DEGRADED
- SAFE_MODE
- RECOVERY

---

# M

## MEMORY

The subsystem responsible for:
- semantic persistence
- continuity reconstruction
- anchor stabilization

---

## MODULE

An isolated functional subsystem with:
- explicit responsibilities
- declared dependencies
- recoverable execution behavior

---

# O

## OBSERVABILITY

The capability to inspect:
- execution states
- transitions
- failures
- synchronization behavior

Critical operations must remain observable.

---

## ORCHESTRATION

The coordination layer responsible for:
- execution sequencing
- synchronization
- lifecycle management
- recovery coordination

---

# P

## PERSISTENCE

The act of storing validated semantic state
for future reconstruction or continuity preservation.

Unvalidated persistence is forbidden.

---

## PROVIDER

An external or internal inference engine
used for semantic generation or computation.

Providers require:
- validation
- isolation
- orchestration supervision

---

# R

## RECOVERY

A controlled reconstruction process
used to stabilize degraded execution states.

Recovery priorities:
1. integrity
2. containment
3. stabilization
4. reintegration

---

## RECURSION

Self-referential execution behavior.

Allowed recursion must remain:
- bounded
- monitored
- recoverable

Unbounded recursion is prohibited.

---

## ROUTING

The subsystem responsible for:
- execution classification
- provider selection
- orchestration path determination

---

# S

## SAFE MODE

A constrained operational state activated
during severe instability or corruption.

Objectives:
- contain instability
- preserve recoverability
- suppress escalation

---

## SCORING

The subsystem responsible for:
- confidence evaluation
- semantic validation
- contradiction analysis

---

## SEMANTIC INTEGRITY

The preservation of coherent meaning,
contextual consistency,
and contradiction resistance.

---

## SESSION

A bounded continuity window representing
an active orchestration interaction period.

---

## STATE MACHINE

The lifecycle control subsystem responsible for:
- validated transitions
- synchronization sequencing
- execution state regulation

---

## SYNCHRONIZATION

The process of aligning:
- memory
- context
- orchestration states
- semantic anchors

Synchronization failures may trigger degradation.

---

# T

## TERMINATION

The irreversible shutdown of execution activity.

Termination may occur through:
- graceful completion
- governance intervention
- failsafe enforcement

---

# V

## VALIDATION

The process of verifying:
- semantic consistency
- continuity alignment
- orchestration safety
- corruption resistance

Validation is mandatory before persistence.

---

Core philosophy:

"Stable cognition depends on
shared semantic discipline."
